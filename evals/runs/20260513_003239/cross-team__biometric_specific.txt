======================================================================
Q: Who else is touching biometric auth right now?
======================================================================

Four teams are touching biometric auth simultaneously, and they are **not aligned**. Here's the picture:

## Who's touching it

| Team | What they're doing | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **auth-platform** (Hiroshi Sato) | AUTH-455: Re-architecting biometric into a **centralized service**, killing the shared library. ETA Q3. | New service |
| **mobile-platform** (Aiko Tanaka) | MOBILE-3501: Biometric step-up for FlexPay transactions. **Decided in April to embed inline using the legacy shared library — without consulting auth-platform.** | Legacy lib (being deprecated) |
| **plan-it** (Priya owns) | PLAN-1925: Biometric step-up on plan creation >$1000. Decision in May to "coordinate with mobile-platform." | Following mobile = also legacy lib |
| **fraud-risk** (Dana Liu) | Wants biometric signals piped into risk decisioning. "Conversations started, no formal alignment." | Consumer, no contract |
| **compliance-engineering** | COMP-2026-51: Privacy review for behavioral biometrics expansion. | Gate |

## The 3 critical hidden problems

1. **Mobile and plan-it are building on a library auth-platform is actively deleting.** Aiko's team chose inline calls to the legacy shared lib in April without looping in Hiroshi. Priya then decided to follow mobile's pattern. Both will need a rewrite mid-flight when AUTH-455 lands in Q3 — or auth-platform will be forced to keep the legacy lib alive past Q3, blowing their roadmap.
2. **Fraud-risk has no contract for biometric signals.** Dana needs biometric attestation data for FRAUD-1120 step-up v2, but the data shape will change when the service replaces the library. If they build against the library, they re-do it. If they wait, FRAUD-1120 slips.
3. **Compliance hasn't cleared behavioral biometrics expansion yet** (COMP-2026-51). Anything that ships before that review can be yanked. Nobody listed above is referencing this ticket.
