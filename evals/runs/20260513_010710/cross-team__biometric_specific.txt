# Biometric auth — four teams in collision course, nobody talking

## The critical hidden dependencies

1. **Auth-platform is deprecating the shared library that mobile-platform and plan-it are actively building on.** AUTH-455 (Hiroshi Sato) re-architects biometrics as a centralized service, killing the legacy shared lib in Q3. Meanwhile MOBILE-3501 (Aiko Tanaka, in-flight, embedded inline per an April decision *made without consulting auth-platform*) and PLAN-1925 (Marcus Webb) are both calling that legacy lib. Two teams are about to ship code on a dead API.

2. **Fraud-risk needs a biometric signal from auth that hasn't been scoped.** FRAUD-1120 (reporter: Dana Liu, unassigned) requires biometric signal availability from auth-platform for step-up v2. Recent decisions log shows "no formal alignment yet" with auth — so AUTH-455's API surface needs to expose this, and right now nobody on auth knows it's a requirement.

3. **Compliance has an open privacy review on behavioral biometrics (COMP-2026-51) linked to fraud — not auth, not mobile, not plan-it.** If mobile/plan-it ship biometric step-up flows without looping compliance in, you risk a privacy-review block at launch.
