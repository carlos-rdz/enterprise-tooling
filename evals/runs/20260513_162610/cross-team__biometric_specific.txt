# Biometric auth — who's in the blast radius

Four teams shipping biometric work simultaneously, plus a compliance review. There's a clear collision course.

## The critical hidden dependencies

1. **Legacy lib is being deprecated Q3 — and two teams are actively building against it.**
   - `AUTH-455` (Hiroshi Sato, auth-platform): re-architecting biometric into a centralized service, killing the shared library this quarter.
   - `MOBILE-3501` (Aiko Tanaka): integrating FaceID into FlexPay using the **legacy** shared lib. Mobile-platform's recent decision log explicitly says they chose inline calls "without consulting auth-platform."
   - `PLAN-1925` (Marcus Webb, reported by Priya Shankar): plan-it adding biometric step-up >$1000, also riding the legacy path. Priya at least flagged "coordinate with mobile-platform on biometric" in May — but neither team has looped Hiroshi in.

2. **Fraud is waiting on a biometric signal contract that doesn't exist yet.**
   - `FRAUD-1120` (Dana Liu, unassigned): step-up auth v2 needs biometric signal from auth-platform. Fraud-risk's own decision log: *"Started conversations with auth-platform... No formal alignment yet."* If AUTH-455's new service API doesn't expose what fraud needs, FRAUD-1120 slips.

3. **Compliance has a parallel privacy review running.**
   - `COMP-2026-51`: privacy review for behavioral biometrics expansion, linked to fraud — not to the mobile/plan-it step-up work. If FlexPay step-up flows aren't scoped into this review, you'll get a UDAAP/privacy surprise (and COMP-2026-44 UDAAP review for FlexPay Standard is "not started").
